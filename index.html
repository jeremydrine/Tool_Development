Use Set reference zero with the phone on a known plane on the car or a known level/vertical reference. Then move the phone to the wheel surface to read relative wheel lean.

Quick workflow

  1. Park on the flattest surface you can manage.
  2. Tap Start sensors and allow motion access.
  3. Place the phone on a reference plane and tap Set reference zero.
  4. Measure and capture camber at each wheel.
  5. For caster, steer to equal left/right angles and capture both readings.
  6. For toe, use strings or straight edges and type in your front/rear gaps.

The phone gives you good tilt data. Toe still needs an external direction reference, and caster still needs a known steering angle.

Important: This is a field tool, not a replacement for a professional alignment rack. It is strongest for camber, useful for caster with a good steering-angle reference, and useful for toe only when paired with strings or straight edges.
